Sciences Skill Tree

Math 10C skills are foundational and serve as a basis for more advanced mathematics courses in high school and beyond. Math 10C aims to provide students with a solid understanding of fundamental mathematical concepts and problem-solving skills that will be useful in a variety of contexts, both in further education and in everyday life.
Math 20-1 is designed to prepare students for more advanced math courses, including Math 30-1 and university-level mathematics courses. The skills acquired in this course are valuable for various fields and are essential for students pursuing careers in STEM (science, technology, engineering, and mathematics) and related disciplines.
Math 30-1 is a demanding course that equips students with the mathematical foundation needed for higher-level math and science courses at the university level. It emphasizes critical thinking, abstract reasoning, and advanced mathematical concepts, making it an essential step for students pursuing STEM-related careers or higher education in mathematics or science.
English 10-1 is designed to prepare students for more advanced English and literature courses. The skills acquired in this course are valuable for effective communication, critical thinking, and success in various academic and professional endeavors.
Students in English 20-1 deepen their understanding of literature and language through advanced literary analysis, complex essay writing, and critical thinking. They gain proficiency in analyzing and interpreting texts, including novels, poetry, and drama, exploring more sophisticated literary themes and techniques. Essay writing becomes more intricate, with a focus on crafting well-structured arguments, synthesizing research, and utilizing advanced vocabulary and language.
English 30-1 is designed to challenge students to think critically, communicate effectively, and analyze literature and language in a profound manner. It equips them with advanced skills that are valuable not only for further academic studies but also for professional success in fields that require strong communication and critical thinking abilities.
Social Studies 10-1 aims to prepare students for a world where they can critically analyze complex social issues, participate in civic life, and appreciate the diversity of cultures and ideas. It provides a foundation for further social studies courses and helps students become informed, engaged, and responsible members of their communities and the global society.
Social Studies 20-1 aims to deepen students' understanding of the world's complexities, encouraging them to think critically, engage with global issues, and become responsible and informed citizens. It prepares them for more advanced social studies courses and helps them develop a well-rounded perspective on society, culture, and politics in an increasingly interconnected world.
Social Studies 30-1 aims to equip students with the knowledge and skills to critically analyze, evaluate, and engage in discussions about complex social, political, and cultural issues. It prepares students for further studies in social sciences and humanities and encourages them to become informed, engaged, and responsible citizens who contribute to their communities and society.
Chemistry 10-1 aims to provide students with a fundamental understanding of chemistry and to introduce them to important concepts and skills for further studies in chemistry or related fields. It emphasizes problem-solving, laboratory skills, and critical thinking, and it encourages students to apply chemical principles to real-world situations.
Chemistry 20-1 challenges students to think critically and analytically about chemical concepts, equipping them with a strong foundation for further studies in chemistry and related fields at the post-secondary level. The course emphasizes problem-solving, laboratory skills, and the application of chemical principles in real-world situations.
Chemistry 30-1 challenges students to think critically and analytically about complex chemical concepts, equipping them with a strong foundation for further studies in chemistry, engineering, and the physical sciences. The course emphasizes problem-solving, laboratory skills, and the application of chemical principles in real-world scenarios, preparing students for careers in scientific research, industry, and related fields.
Physics 10-1 serves as a foundation for more advanced physics courses in high school and beyond. It provides students with a basic understanding of the physical laws that govern the natural world and helps them develop problem-solving and analytical skills. It can also be a starting point for those interested in pursuing further studies in physics or related fields.
Physics 20-1 provides a strong foundation for further studies in physics, engineering, and related fields at the post-secondary level. It equips students with a deeper understanding of the physical laws that govern the universe and fosters analytical and problem-solving skills essential for success in scientific and technical fields.
Physics 30-1 provides a strong foundation for students pursuing higher education in physics, engineering, and related fields. It challenges students to think deeply and analytically, preparing them for academic and professional pursuits in scientific and technical disciplines.
Biology 10-1 serves as a foundation for more advanced biology courses and provides students with a broad understanding of biological concepts. It also helps students develop critical thinking and scientific inquiry skills, which are valuable for both further education in the life sciences and for making informed decisions in various aspects of life.
Biology 20-1 aims to provide students with a strong foundation in biology and prepare them for more advanced courses in the life sciences or for pursuing careers in biology-related fields. The course emphasizes critical thinking, experimental skills, and a deep understanding of biological concepts, allowing students to explore and analyze the complexities of the living world.
Biology 30-1 challenges students to think critically and analytically, allowing them to explore complex biological concepts in-depth. It aims to prepare students for advanced studies in the life sciences and related fields at the post-secondary level and for careers in research, healthcare, environmental science, and more. The course emphasizes the application of biological principles and ethical considerations in the context of modern biology.
